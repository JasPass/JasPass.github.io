Mathematics

Linear Algebra
  • Description: Einsteins theory of gravity, in which the world is modelled as a 4-dimensional Lorentzian manifold, equiped with a metric structure.

Real Analysis
  • Description: The consitent scheme for combining quantum mechanics and special relativity, by quantizing classical field theories; the foundation of the entire Standard Model.

Complex Function Theory
  • Description: The study of consistently combining Quantum Mechanics and General Relativity; String Theory, Loop Quantum Gravity and Causal Dynamical Triangulation are all attempts at QG.

Fluid Dynamics
  • Description: The branch og classical mechanics, concerned with the study of systems with many degrees of freedom, by approximating these systems as continous media, described by fields.

Condensed Matter Theory
  • Description: The study of how our elementary theories of physics give rise to new emergent properties in systems with a large number of particles.

Statistical Physics
  • Description: The link between our microscopic physical theories and macroscopic effective theory of thermodynamics, through the use of statistical analysis.

String Theory
  • Description: Initially the study of Quantized Relativistic Strings, as a way to produce spin-2 particles; Central to Holography and some modern approaches to Scattering Amplitudes.

Quantum Mechanics
  • Description: The foundation of our current understanding of physics, in which the states of any system are elements of a Hilbert space and observables are operators on this Hilbert space.

Classical Mechanics
  • Description: The origion of modern physics, starting with the formulation by Isaac Newton and later reformulated in the work of Joseph-Louis Lagrange and Sir William Rowan Hamilton.
